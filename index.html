ASRC Statistics Page

The Asylum Seeker Resource Centre has provided a statistics on Australian and worldwide refugee and asylum seeker populations. There is a large amount of data available to do this and can provide data on; where the largest populations of refugees and asylum seekers are found, how Australia compares to the rest of the world and how many people are locked up in detention and how many children are locked up in detention. Requests for the latest statistics have come from, concerned members of the public, journalists and other asylum seeker and refugee service providers.

Data was sourced from:

  • UNHCR Global Trends 2013,
  • UNHCR Mid Year Asylum Trends 2014, and
  • DIBP Monthly Immigration Detention Statistics.

The following were used in the design:

  • WordPress,
  • HTML, CSS and JavaScript,
  • Google Visualisation, and
  • Google Spreadsheets

Stand Up to Protect Campaign

The Asylum Seeker Resource Centre ran a campaign to prevent the Resolving the Asylum Legacy Caseload Bill 2014 passing the Australian Senate.

My role in the campaigns team, amongst others, was to create a set of graphics that focused on the areas the passing of this bill would create for people in detention if passed.

The set of graphics were design to easily be sharable and to engage the audience, whether they were supporters or not, to the potential risks of the bill passing.

Timeline of Asylum Seeker and Refugee Events

This timeline was created to show the biggest events, policy or otherwise, in the asylum seeker and refugee world since 2012.

As this can be seen as some educational material, there is going to be a large amount of text and slabs of text throughout the page. To break up this text, multimedia, such as photos, videos and quotes and other media such as tweets needed to be implemented to fit within the timleine box.

The timeline was created with HTML and designed with CSS, this allows the timeline to easily be implemented within the ASRC's WordPress template and can easily be updated as more events will happen.

As the timleine grows with information, there will be a need for greater interactivity that allows the user to sort events by categories, such as years or government policies.

Myths, Facts and Solutions

Thorough research and a huge attention to detail is required in the writing and editing of this document that has been used by a range of different people in different professions.

As with other ASRC educational materials, my responsibility with this document lies with overseeing the research and writing of the other team members and the print and design layout of the final version.
